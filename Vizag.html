The fate of Hindustan Petroleum Corp Ltd-promoted $10 billion Vizag refinery-cum-petrochemical project is uncertain. Petroleum minister Murli Deora has told the Parliament that consortium led by state-run HPCL would review feasibility of the project in the light of the current economic slowdown. The other members of the consortium are: LN Mittal group, French oil company TOTAL S.A. and public sector firms GAIL India and Oil India Ltd (OIL). "Due to melting down of the global economy and unprecedented reduction in demand of petroleum products, the viability to proceed with the feasibility study of this project will be once again reviewed in the month of April-May 2009. The consent from consortium partners are also still awaited to proceed with the feasibility study in view of the current economic situation," Mr Deora told the Rajya Sabha on Tuesday. Mr Deora said that only after establishing that the project was "techno economically viable and the consortium partners agree to proceed with the next phase of study of the project, HPCL would be able to work out details pertaining to employment creation and the assistance required from the state government for this project." In October 2007, HPCL had signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with GAIL, OIL, TOTAL and Mittal Energy Investments Pte Ltd to explore the possibility for setting up a 14-15 million metric tonne per annum (MMTPA) capacity refinery-cum-petrochemical-project in the proposed petroleum, chemicals and petrochemicals investment region (PCPIR) near Visakhapatnam.
